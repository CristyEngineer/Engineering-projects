# ⚡ Optimization of the Medium‑Voltage Route Design Process
Process Improvement & Engineering Optimization in Underground Energy Infrastructure

This repository contains a discription of a professional engineering project focused on optimizing the medium‑voltage (MV) route design process within a large contractor operating in the underground energy infrastructure sector.

# 🎯 Project Summary
The goal of this project was to analyze and improve the workflow used to design medium‑voltage cable routes. The existing process showed inefficiencies, long lead times and inconsistent design quality. Using engineering methods and structured process analysis, the project delivered:

- A streamlined and standardized MV route design workflow
- Clear design criteria and decision rules
- Reduced rework and improved predictability
- Better alignment between engineering, field operations and project management

The optimized process supports faster project preparation and more reliable execution in the field.

# 🧩 Methods & Approach
The project followed a structured engineering and process‑optimization approach:

- Process mapping & bottleneck analysis
- Stakeholder interviews
- Requirements and constraints analysis
- Design criteria definition
- Evaluation of alternative workflows
- Selection of the preferred design method
- Development of a standardized MV route design process

# 📊 Key Outcomes
- Reduced design time through clearer steps and decision logic
- Improved quality and consistency of MV route designs
- Better integration of field constraints into early‑stage engineering
- A scalable workflow suitable for future growth and digitalization
